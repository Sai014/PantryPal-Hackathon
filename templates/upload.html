Food wastage in restaurants is a major global issue, contributing to both economic losses and environmental damage. Every day, tons of perfectly edible food end up in landfills due to overproduction, portion sizes that are too large, and inefficient inventory management. Restaurants often discard surplus ingredients, unsold meals, and leftovers, leading to unnecessary waste. This not only increases operational costs but also contributes to greenhouse gas emissions as food decomposes and releases methane. By adopting better planning, portion control, and food donation programs, restaurants can significantly reduce waste, save money, and help fight hunger. Implementing sustainable practices, such as composting and utilizing food scraps creatively, can also make a huge difference in reducing the environmental impact of food wastage.
